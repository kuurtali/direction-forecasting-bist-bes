Literature Review
From classical time-series to deep sequence models
Classical ARIMA
Box-Jenkins ARIMA remains the dominant benchmark in pension-fund return forecasting. KaradaÄŸ Erdemir & KÄ±rkaÄŸaÃ§ (2024) applied ARIMA to Turkish BES pension funds. EÅŸsiz & Ordu (2024) applied ARIMA to S&P 500 fund baskets. Strong parametric baseline, blind to non-linear structure.
Deep Learning in Finance
Fischer & Krauss (2018) showed LSTM networks outperform memory-free models on S&P 500 constituents. Siami-Namini et al. (2019) reported LSTM/BiLSTM improvements over ARIMA on economic time-series. 1D-CNN offers a competitive and computationally cheaper alternative (Van der Burgt, 2023).
Evaluation Methodology Gap
Finance DL studies frequently report raw accuracy without specificity, sensitivity or baseline comparison. Majority-Class illusion (Spec=0 or Sens=0) is rarely flagged, leading to overstated predictive performance on imbalanced data. This study addresses this gap explicitly.

MC Illusion Mechanism
When Specificity = 0 (model never predicts Down):
BA = (Sensitivity + 0) / 2 â‰ˆ 50%
Raw Acc = 100% but BA = 50.0% â€” coin flip.
(Valverde-Albacete & Pelaez-Moreno, 2014)
Why BA Reveals the Illusion
BA = (Sens + Spec) / 2 = (1.0 + 0.0) / 2 = 0.50
A random classifier scores the same.
Conclusion: DL learns nothing on ALZ.
Control Group Role
ALZ confirms: with monotonic low-risk funds, class imbalance is so extreme that any binary model collapses to single-class prediction regardless of feature set.
